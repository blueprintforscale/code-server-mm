### Key Topics
*   **Transition from ServiceMonster to Housecall Pro** Update
    *   Karina reported that the team had begun transitioning to Housecall Pro, noting that it is more intuitive and modern compared to ServiceMonster, especially with its improved pipeline system and customer portal.
    *   She highlighted issues experienced during the transition, such as data misuploads caused by multiple divisions and the inability to send individual emails directly from customer profiles—a feature they frequently used in ServiceMonster.
    *   The limitations have prompted the team to explore workarounds, including using Zapier with Outlook to enable individual email functionality.
    
*   **Integration between Housecall Pro and GoHighLevel (GHL)** Decision
    *   The discussion centered on leveraging GHL to supplement Housecall Pro's missing functionalities, particularly for handling immediate responses to missed calls and form submissions.
    *   The group agreed to use GHL for initiating contact with leads, as Housecall Pro lacks the flexibility for customized and immediate automated responses compared to GHL.
    *   It was emphasized that except for the immediate touchpoints, all other automated messaging in GHL should be turned off to prevent duplicate messaging with Housecall Pro.
    
*   **Call Tracking Transition from CallRail to GHL** Decision
    *   Hayk and Susie discussed consolidating call tracking by transitioning from CallRail to GHL, with a plan to set up new phone pool numbers in GHL.
    *   A transitional period of one to two months was proposed to monitor whether the old CallRail numbers continue to receive traffic before fully porting or canceling them.
    *   The team considered using temporary new numbers, updating marketing channels (such as Google Ads), and later porting numbers if necessary, balancing operational continuity with system consolidation.
    
*   **Review Request Integration** Idea
    *   The group evaluated whether to manage review requests in Housecall Pro or GHL.
    *   Susie recommended using GHL for review requests due to its greater flexibility in customization, including leveraging landing pages to gatekeep reviews.
    *   The possibility of using Zapier to integrate Housecall Pro with GHL for review request automation was discussed, allowing a job marked complete in Housecall Pro to trigger a review sequence in GHL.
    
*   **Managing and Consolidating Messaging Workflows** Decision
    *   The conversation stressed the importance of consolidating messaging workflows to prevent overlapping communications between Housecall Pro and GHL.
    *   Karina expressed the need to retain only immediate response messaging from GHL (for missed calls and form submissions) while deactivating other automated workflows to ensure clarity and consistency in customer communications.
    *   This consolidation was deemed critical for achieving a streamlined and effective communication process across the two platforms.
    
*   **Next Steps and Action Items** Decision
    *   Karina planned to consult with her team, particularly with Jessica, to finalize the consolidation strategy and obtain internal approval for the proposed changes.
    *   Hayk mentioned that Susie would provide an estimate for transitioning call tracking from CallRail to GHL, which includes setting up new phone pool numbers, embedding tracking scripts, and performing test calls with RingCentral integration.
    *   Susie indicated that she would immediately start pausing non-critical GHL workflows to ensure that only the desired messaging remains active.
    *   The team agreed to monitor the performance of the new call tracking setup over the coming month or two, after which they would either cancel CallRail or port the numbers if necessary.
