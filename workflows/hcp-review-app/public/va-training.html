What is the Lead Review tool?

Every lead that comes in through Google Ads or LSA gets matched to a customer record in our field management software (Housecall Pro). The Lead Review tool flags leads that need a human to verify the data looks correct. Your job is to review each flagged lead, resolve any issues, and either approve it or flag it for a manager.

1. Understanding the Layout

Top Bar

  • Client dropdown — Select "All Clients — Review Queue" to see flagged leads across all clients, or pick a specific client to see just their leads.
  • Need Review button (red) — Shows the count of leads waiting for your review. This is your main work queue.
  • All Leads — Shows every lead (reviewed and unreviewed). Useful for searching.
  • Approved — Shows leads that have already been reviewed and approved. Faded out.

Progress Bar

Shows how many leads you've reviewed vs. how many remain. The green bar fills up as you work through the queue.

Lead Cards

Each card shows a lead summary. Click it to expand and see the full detail.

2. Filters & Navigation

When you first load the page with "All Clients" selected, it automatically shows the Need Review queue. These are leads the system has flagged for human review.

Use the Prev/Next buttons at the bottom to page through results (25 leads per page).

Tip: Work top to bottom

Leads are sorted so the ones with the most issues appear first. Work from top to bottom for maximum impact.

3. Reading a Lead Card

Card Summary (Collapsed)

Before you click, each card shows:

  • Name — The customer's name from Housecall Pro
  • Source badge — Google Ads or LSA or google_business_profile
  • Status badge — Issue means it needs attention. Approved means done. Flagged means a manager needs to look.
  • Date, phone, match method — When the lead came in, their phone number, and how they were matched (phone, email, etc.)
  • Revenue — The ROAS revenue amount in green. A dash (—) means no revenue yet.

Red left border = has issues

Cards with a red left border have flagged issues. These are the ones that need your attention most.

Card Detail (Expanded)

Click a card to expand it. You'll see:

  1. Needs Attention box (red box at top) — Lists exactly what's wrong in plain English. This tells you why the lead was flagged.
  2. Lead Info — Phone, email, source, lead date
  3. Pipeline — How far the lead has progressed: inspections, estimates, jobs, invoices
  4. Inspections section — Each inspection with date, address, status, and amount
  5. Estimates section — Each estimate with date, address, status, and amount
  6. Jobs section — Each job with date, address, status, and amount
  7. Invoices section — Each invoice with date, number, status, and amount
  8. Lead Source selector — Change the attribution if needed
  9. Action buttons — Approve, Flag, or Skip

4. Understanding Issue Types

Structural Issues (shown in red boxes)

"Multiple inspections at different addresses"

This customer has inspections at more than one address. Each address is a separate project and should stay separate. This is usually fine — just verify and approve.

"Multiple inspections at same address"

This customer has multiple inspections at the same address. These might be segments of one project (e.g., attic + crawlspace done on different days) and may need to be grouped together.

"Multiple estimates — may need to be combined"

This customer has more than one estimate. They could be options for the same project (e.g., basic vs. premium remediation plan) or separate projects.

"Multiple jobs at same/different addresses"

Same logic as inspections. Same address = possible segments to group. Different addresses = separate projects, keep as-is.

"Job found but $0 revenue — amount may be missing"

There's an active job but the dollar amount is zero. The job total might not have been entered in Housecall Pro yet. Flag this for the manager.

Data Quality Issues (from system flags)

"Missing funnel step"

The lead is missing a step in the normal flow. For example, they have a job but no inspection, or an estimate but no inspection. This could mean a record wasn't created in HCP, or the lead was handled differently. Review and approve if it makes sense, or flag if something looks off.

"Job exists without an estimate"

There's a job but no estimate was sent. Some clients skip estimates for smaller jobs — this can be normal. If the job has a reasonable amount and the rest of the lead looks good, approve it.

"Segment revenue looks off"

The revenue on a segment (part of a grouped job) doesn't look right compared to other segments or the total. Check the individual amounts. Flag for manager if unsure.

"Classification uncertain"

The system couldn't confidently determine if a record is an inspection or a job. Look at the description, the amount, and the timing. If a "job" is actually a $0 inspection, you can reclassify it using the type pill.

"Name mismatch"

The name from the call tracking system (CallRail) doesn't match the name in HCP. This is common — caller ID often shows a different name than the actual customer. As long as the phone number matched, it's usually fine.

"Pre-existing customer" (amber Pre-lead pill)

The customer's record in HCP/Jobber was created more than 7 days before their first Google Ads call. This means they were already a customer before any ad brought them in. Their revenue might not be attributable to Google Ads — they may have found the company through a referral or previous visit, then later clicked an ad. Flag for manager unless the dates are very close (within a couple weeks) and it looks like a normal new customer flow.

5. Actions You Can Take

Main Actions (bottom of card)

Approve — Looks Good

Use this when everything looks correct. The lead, pipeline, and revenue all make sense. The card disappears from your queue.

Flag for Manager

Use this when something doesn't look right and you're not sure what to do. You can add a note explaining what you noticed. A manager will review it later.

Skip

Closes the card without taking action. The lead stays in the queue. Use this if you need to come back to it later.

Section Actions (for structural issues)

When a section (inspections, estimates, or jobs) has a red border and shows multiple items, you'll see these options:

  • Group Selected — Select 2+ items by clicking them (they turn blue), then click this button to mark them as parts of one project. The first item becomes the "parent" and the others become nested segments/options.
  • All separate → Keep as-is — Click this if each item is a genuinely separate project (e.g., different addresses, different customers at same address). This resolves the issue.
  • Not sure → Flag — If you can't tell whether items should be grouped, flag the section for a manager.

Auto-approve

Once all issue sections are resolved (either grouped, kept separate, or flagged), the lead automatically approves and removes itself from your queue. You don't need to click Approve separately.

Other Actions

  • Revenue toggle (checkbox) — Uncheck to exclude a record from revenue calculations. Use this if an item is a duplicate or shouldn't count toward revenue.
  • Type pill (inspection ↔ / job ↔) — Click to reclassify a record. A confirmation will pop up. Use this if an inspection is actually a job or vice versa.
  • Lead Source selector — Change where the lead is attributed. Only change this if you have a reason to (e.g., it says "Unknown" but you can see it came from Google Ads).
  • Undo (on grouped items) — If you grouped items by mistake, click "Undo" on the group label to split them back apart.

6. Common Scenarios

Easy

Lead looks clean, no red boxes

The "Needs Attention" box says something like "Missing funnel step" but when you look at the pipeline, there's an inspection, estimate, and job — all with reasonable amounts.

1
Verify the pipeline makes sense (inspection → estimate → job)
2
Check that revenue is reasonable (not $0 if there's a completed job)
3
Click Approve
Easy

Multiple inspections at different addresses

Customer has 2 inspections — one at "123 Main St" and one at "456 Oak Ave".

1
Confirm the addresses are actually different
2
Click All separate → Keep as-is
3
Lead auto-approves
Medium

Multiple inspections at the same address

Customer has 2 inspections both at "123 Main St" — one on Jan 5 and one on Jan 12.

1
Look at the dates. If they're close together (within a few weeks), they're probably segments of one project (e.g., attic inspection + crawlspace inspection)
2
Click both items to select them (they turn blue)
3
Click Group Selected
4
Lead auto-approves

Exception: If inspections are months apart, they might be separate visits (e.g., re-inspection after treatment). In that case, Keep as-is.

Medium

Multiple estimates for one customer

Customer has 2 estimates — "$4,500 Mold Remediation" and "$6,200 Mold Remediation + Encapsulation".

1
Check if they're for the same address. If yes, they're probably options (basic vs. premium plan)
2
Select both estimates, then click Group Selected. They become options under one estimate.
3
If one is approved and the other isn't, the approved one's revenue is what counts
Medium

Job with $0 revenue

The lead has a job marked "Scheduled" or "In Progress" but the amount is $0.

1
Check if there's an estimate with a dollar amount — if yes, the revenue comes from the estimate and this is fine
2
If there's no estimate either, Flag for Manager with a note: "Job has $0 and no estimate"
Medium

Classification uncertain — is it an inspection or a job?

A record is labeled "job" but it's $0 and was done before any estimate was sent.

1
Look at the timeline: if it happened before an estimate, it's probably an inspection
2
Look at the description: if it says "mold inspection" or "assessment," it's an inspection
3
Click the job ↔ pill to reclassify it as an inspection
Easy

Name mismatch

CallRail says "SMITH JOHN" but HCP says "John Smith". Or CallRail says "ACME PLUMBING" but HCP says "Sarah Johnson".

1
This is almost always fine — caller ID names are unreliable
2
As long as the phone number matched, click Approve
3
Only flag if the lead looks like it might be a completely different person (different phone AND different name AND different date)
Advanced

Lead source looks wrong

A lead shows "Unknown" source but you can see it came from a Google Ads call.

1
Look at the Lead Info section for clues — does it have a GCLID? Does the source field say anything?
2
If you're confident about the correct source, use the Lead Source selector at the bottom to change it
3
If unsure, leave it and approve — the source can always be corrected later
Advanced

Multiple jobs at the same address with different amounts

Customer has Job 1 ($3,200) and Job 2 ($1,800) both at "123 Main St", one week apart.

1
These are likely segments — the client split the work into two parts (e.g., demo + remediation)
2
Select both, click Group Selected
3
The total revenue ($5,000) is now counted as one project

Exception: If jobs are months apart at the same address, it might be a separate project (e.g., return customer). Keep as-is in that case.

7. Decision Rules

When to Approve

  • The pipeline makes sense (inspection → estimate → job → invoice is the normal flow)
  • Revenue amounts are reasonable and non-zero (for completed jobs)
  • All structural issues have been resolved (grouped or kept separate)
  • Minor flags like name mismatch or classification — you've verified it looks correct

When to Flag for Manager

  • You're not sure whether items should be grouped or kept separate
  • Revenue is $0 with no estimate and a completed/scheduled job
  • Something looks genuinely wrong that you can't fix (e.g., wrong customer entirely)
  • The lead seems like it might be spam or a wrong number, but you're not certain

When to Skip

  • You need more context and want to come back later
  • You're in the middle of something and want to continue with another lead first

Grouping Rules of Thumb

Same address, close dates = Group them

If two inspections, jobs, or estimates are at the same address within a few weeks of each other, they are almost always parts of the same project. Group them.

Different addresses = Keep separate

Different addresses always mean different projects. Click "Keep as-is."

Same address, months apart = Usually keep separate

If visits to the same address are several months apart, it's likely a return customer or a new project. Keep as-is, or flag if you're unsure.

8. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Don't approve without expanding the card. Always click to see the detail before approving. The card summary doesn't show everything.
  • Don't group items at different addresses. Different addresses = different projects, always.
  • Don't change the lead source unless you're sure. If it says "Google Ads" and you're not certain it's wrong, leave it alone.
  • Don't uncheck revenue toggles unless you know why. Unchecking a revenue toggle removes that amount from the client's ROAS calculation. Only do this for genuine duplicates.
  • Don't spend too long on one lead. If you've been looking at a lead for more than 2 minutes and you're still unsure, flag it for the manager and move on.
  • Don't worry about name mismatches. Caller ID names are unreliable. The phone number match is what matters.

Remember: Flag is always safe

If you're ever unsure about anything, flagging for a manager is always the right call. It's better to flag something that turns out to be fine than to approve something that's wrong. A manager can always approve a flagged lead, but fixing an incorrectly approved lead is harder.
