### Key Topics
*   **Pipeline Update Functionality & Duplicate Estimates** Update
    *   The team reviewed the new pipeline update that was fully installed and ready for use, focusing on its ability to handle both dispatch (by Jill) and estimate closing (by Ethan) requirements.
    *   Discussion centered on managing when a customer receives more than one estimate. Ethan highlighted the issue of duplicate names appearing in the pipeline and suggested options such as using notes or custom fields (e.g., turning off automatic messages on one duplicate) to differentiate multiple estimates.
    *   It was noted that the system is contact-based rather than job-based, which avoids sending duplicate automated messages to the same contact.
    
*   **Field Customization & Technical Glitches** Problem
    *   The disappearing 'contacted' field was discussed; users were instructed to click 'Manage Fields' upon login to restore it temporarily for the day until a permanent fix is found.
    *   Issues were raised about drag-and-drop glitches and sorting inconsistencies in the pipeline (e.g., cards moving back to the wrong column such as evaluation scheduled reverting from evaluation completed).
    *   The team acknowledged these glitches and the need for ongoing adjustments to ensure smooth workflow.
    
*   **Mobile App Performance & Phone Consult Integration** Update
    *   The mobile app version of Blueprint was evaluated, with concerns raised about slow navigation and freezing issues during use, particularly when driving or on the go.
    *   A customized, simplified version of the mobile app was mentioned, which will be sent to testers for feedback.
    *   Integration of phone consults into the system was discussed, including scheduling for follow-up calls and how changes in pipeline status (such as 'scheduled service plan' or 'needs callback') trigger text notifications.
    *   There was also discussion on the sorting and display of call logs, ensuring that new calls and follow-up dates appear correctly.
    
*   **Automation Workflows & Drip Sequences** Update
    *   The team examined how automation workflows work for lost and abandoned leads using drip sequences—automated text messages and emails are sent to prompt follow-ups.
    *   Triggering of drip sequences by inputting the word or emoji 'drip' in a lost reason was reviewed, as well as how subsequent emails/texts are scheduled (e.g., a 30-minute delay before the follow-up email after the initial text).
    *   The nuances between automating follow-ups for estimates versus general inspection leads were discussed, with emphasis on consolidating lost reasons to simplify data reporting.
    
*   **Review Request Mechanism** Update
    *   The review request feature was demonstrated, showing how a text message prompts customers to leave a review.
    *   An issue was noted where the review link opened a blank page, prompting discussion on updating the messaging template to provide a confirmation message after a review is submitted.
    *   Team members experimented with the review function to ensure that both positive feedback is captured and that customers are appropriately thanked.
    
*   **User Management & Permissions** Update
    *   The process for adding new users (e.g., Nick) and copying permissions was reviewed.
    *   There was a discussion on granting full administrative rights to certain users (keywords like "keys to the kingdom") and setting up temporary passwords if necessary.
    *   The importance of consistent internal naming (e.g., grouping Ethan, Japan, Nick, and Jill) was mentioned for clarity in the system.
    
*   **Integration with HouseCall Pro, Grasshopper, and Call Logging** Problem
    *   Concerns were raised about integration challenges between HouseCall Pro and Blueprint; not all leads (e.g., estimates made by Stacy) automatically populate correctly.
    *   The system was noted to integrate calls from various sources including website ads and Grasshopper, although some discrepancies in call logs and lead data were identified.
    *   Issues with sorting call logs by 'date created' or 'last activity' were also discussed, highlighting the need for tweaks to ensure every lead is captured and visible.
    
*   **Strategic Business Considerations & Lead Generation Performance** Update
    *   The group evaluated overall campaign performance; lead volume has been lower than expected, causing concerns about breaking even.
    *   The discussion included whether additional investment in full phone integration (estimated at ~$300 per month) would be viable if lead generation does not improve.
    *   It was agreed that the campaign’s financial effectiveness and long-term scalability will be closely monitored before making final strategic decisions.
    
*   **Next Steps & Follow-Up Meeting** Decision
    *   Immediate actions include testing the updated mobile app version, fixing the review request template, and addressing integration issues by documenting leads that do not automatically populate.
    *   User management tasks (such as verifying Nick’s account permissions) will be completed.
    *   Team members were advised to monitor the system for recurring glitches, especially with the 'contacted' field.
    *   A follow-up meeting was scheduled for next Thursday (same time) to review system performance, technical updates, and evaluate the need for further full-scale adoption, including phone integration based on improved lead volume.
